What is a TAS?

A Training and Assessment Strategy (TAS) is a mandatory planning document for Australian Registered Training Organizations (RTOs) that outlines how training and assessment will be delivered for a specific qualification.

üìå Key Purpose

The TAS demonstrates to ASQA (Australian Skills Quality Authority) that your training and assessment approach meets the requirements of the relevant training package and complies with the Standards for RTOs 2015.

Why RTOs Need a TAS

  • ASQA Compliance: Mandatory for all qualifications delivered
  • Quality Assurance: Ensures consistency in delivery across trainers
  • Planning Tool: Maps out resources, timing, and assessment methods
  • Audit Evidence: Demonstrates compliance during ASQA audits
  • Continuous Improvement: Provides framework for reviewing and improving delivery

ASQA Compliance Requirements

A compliant TAS must address the following ASQA Standards for RTOs 2015:

Clause Requirement TAS Sections Addressing This
1.1 Training and assessment strategies meet training package requirements Qualification overview, unit clustering, assessment mapping
1.3 Identify and support learner characteristics Cohort profile, LLN support, student support services
1.8 Valid, reliable, fair, and flexible assessment Assessment overview, assessment methods, reasonable adjustments
1.13-1.16 Trainer and assessor competency Trainer requirements, industry currency, professional development
2.2 Continuous improvement Quality assurance, feedback mechanisms, validation processes

Core Concepts

1. Three-Tier Architecture

The system uses a flexible three-tier approach:

Tier 1: TAS Templates

High-level configuration defining structure, formatting, and AI generation settings for different qualification types and AQF levels.

Example: Certificate I General Foundation Skills Template

Tier 2: Template Sections

Reusable content blocks that form the building blocks of TAS documents. Sections exist in a library and can be used across multiple templates.

Examples: Cohort Profile, Assessment Overview, Unit Clustering

Tier 3: Section Assignments

Links between templates and sections, with per-template customization options like ordering, custom labels, and required/optional flags.

Benefit: Same section, different appearance in different templates
